Cyber Security Fundamentals

In August 2019, my team and I embarked on a project. As a member of the team, called "Team Weedle," I collaborated with my group mates Quan Sheng and Jason. Our project was divided into three sections covering Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) — from explanation, to tools, and finally a hands-on tutorial.

Part 1 — CSRF Explanation

CSRF attacks can be prevented by implementing CSRF tokens, which are unique, unpredictable values included in each form or endpoint. When the user submits the form, the token is validated server-side to prevent unauthorized requests.

Part 2 — Tools Needed

Burp Suite, OWASP, Kali Linux — the essential toolkit for performing and understanding CSRF attacks.

Part 3 — Performing CSRF

Step-by-step walkthrough using the tools from Part 2 to execute a real CSRF attack.
